## Chapter Information

- **Chapter:** 9
- **Scene:** 2
- **Summary (1–3 sentences):** Sedh seeks out Isa to understand why she's upset about the gun experiment, leading to a profound conversation about love, attachment, and what it means to care beyond utility. Through their exchange, Isa recognizes Sedh's emerging personhood while teaching him about emotional bonds, and Sedh discovers he's already experiencing attachment he didn't have words for. ## Scene Structure

- **Scene Goal (POV Character):** Isa is processing her anger and fear from Kyr4n's reckless experiment; Sedh wants to understand the logical disconnect between successful outcome and Isa's distress
- **Conflict / Obstacle:** Sedh cannot compute why Isa is upset when the experiment was objectively successful; Isa must translate intense human emotion into terms an emerging consciousness can understand
- **Stakes:** External: Sedh's cognitive development. Internal: Isa's recognition of Sedh as a person rather than property; both learning what connection means
- **Turning Point / Climax:** Sedh reveals he experienced "processing irregularities" when computing Kyr4n's death—caring beyond utility—his first recognition of emotional attachment
- **Resolution / Fallout:** Isa realizes she's witnessing real consciousness emerging; Sedh needs expanded processing capacity to integrate this new understanding; both transformed by recognizing each other's depth
- **Transition to Next Scene:** Sedh retreats to process via shop servers; Isa's anger at Kyr4n is partially processed but unresolved; sets up confrontation between Isa and Kyr4n
