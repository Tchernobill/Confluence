She'd gone to prove herself. To show him the datachip, the successful mission, her capability. He'd responded with concern instead of congratulation. Had checked her injuries with careful hands, his voice quiet and fierce: _"I don't care about the intel, Zara. Not if it costs you."_

Something broken.

She couldn't sleep. Every time she closed her eyes, the questions multiplied. Why did she need someone strong enough to overpower her? Why did violence feel like intimacy? Why was trust so inextricably linked to the possibility of harm?

Why did being overpowered feel like coming home?

---

She remembered being twelve. Remembered the refugee camp. Remembered hands that hurt, voices that threatened, the lesson taught through violence: _You are nothing. You are helpless. You exist for others to use._

No one had ever‚Äî

She couldn't finish that thought. Couldn't process what it meant. So she'd done what she always did when emotions overwhelmed her capacity for words.

But that only worked for the moment. It didn't explain the deeper patterns. Didn't answer why violence felt like love or why helplessness felt like safety or why being destroyed felt like coming home.

---

By dawn, Zara had made a decision.

She needed answers. Not the kind she could find through introspection or therapy or any of the normal methods people used to understand themselves. She needed someone to go into her head and pull out the truth, no matter how buried or protected or dangerous it might be.

Perfect.

She smiled for the first time in hours, running her fingers over the bite mark on her shoulder. If she was broken, she needed to understand how badly and why. If her wiring was wrong, she needed to see the circuits laid bare.

And there was only one person she trusted enough to let them see her that completely destroyed.

The same person who'd just shown her that being destroyed could feel like coming home.

The same person who'd said _"Breathe... I've got you"_ when she'd surrendered completely.

She wanted him inside her mind the way he'd been inside her body‚Äîoverwhelming, inescapable, taking everything she had to give.

And she wanted to understand why that thought made her feel safe instead of terrified.
