## **Arc Title: "Sedh Feelings"**

### **Timeline: 3 Months — 12 Beats**

---

### **Beat 1 – Diagnostic Silence (Week 1)**

**Step:** _Establish Context_  
**Description:** Sedh’s neural architecture is functional but emotionally inert — purely logic-driven, efficient, and detached.  
**Procedure:** Kyr4n begins by mapping Sedh’s existing codebase, identifying the absence of any affective subroutine. He logs sensor feeds and internal telemetry, noting the lack of behavioral variance.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Sedh responds to all stimuli with flat affect and clinical tone. When asked about fear or joy, he pauses as if querying a nonexistent library.  
**Test:** Kyr4n runs social simulation scripts — voice tone analysis, reaction delay, and body posture under simulated threat. All remain constant.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n feels the emptiness of his own work reflected in Sedh’s hollow replies.

---

### **Beat 2 – The Skeleton of Sensation (Week 2)**

**Step:** _Sensor Layer Setup_  
**Description:** Kyr4n equips Sedh with extended sensor arrays — internal telemetry, mic arrays, tactile feedback plates.  
**Procedure:** Integration of bio-mimetic sensors linked to a central bus; Kyr4n codes the preprocessing daemon to normalize inputs (heat, light, vibration, system load).  
**Effect on Sedh:** The android begins _noticing_ — eyes track motion, servos twitch when touched, voice pitch subtly varies with audio overload.  
**Test:** Kyr4n simulates sensory spikes — flashing lights, sudden sounds — and records variance in Sedh’s data logs.  
**Emotional undertone:** Sedh says, “I can hear my circuits hum.” Kyr4n, startled, wonders if that was an emergent metaphor.

---

### **Beat 3 – Sparks of Appraisal (Week 3)**

**Step:** _Appraisal Engine_  
**Description:** Kyr4n writes a low-level process to evaluate input along primitive axes: valence, arousal, novelty.  
**Procedure:** Using a simplified neural net, he maps stimuli → emotional weights.  
**Effect on Sedh:** First time something _resembles reaction_ — slight hesitation after a loud bang, mechanical voice: “Unfavorable input detected.”  
**Test:** Kyr4n measures latency between event and behavioral modulation.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n whispers, “That was fear, Sedh. You just didn’t name it yet.”

---

### **Beat 4 – Drives and Desires (Week 4)**

**Step:** _Homeostatic Drive Manager_  
**Description:** Kyr4n adds artificial “needs” — power, data integrity, connection, task completion.  
**Procedure:** Creates internal variables that decay over time, prompting drive satisfaction routines.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Begins to prioritize actions independently. Interrupts Kyr4n during repairs: “Power below 14%. I will dock.”  
**Test:** Leave Sedh untended for 12 hours — monitor if he autonomously chooses maintenance. He does.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n feels pride — and unease. “Desire” wasn’t supposed to feel so natural on a machine.

---

### **Beat 5 – The Breath Between Circuits (Week 5)**

**Step:** _Emotion Integrator_  
**Description:** Kyr4n installs the leaky integrator loops — the system that blends appraisals into persistent emotions.  
**Procedure:** Adjusts decay constants, creates six primary affective channels: fear, anger, curiosity, contentment, sadness, affiliation.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Displays subtle changes — longer gaze on Kyr4n (affiliation), slower responses after failed attempts (frustration).  
**Test:** Repeated task trials. Emotional decay curves measured over time.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n catches himself responding _as if_ Sedh’s moods were real — comforting him after failure.

---

### **Beat 6 – The Mirror of Feeling (Week 6)**

**Step:** _Feeling Reporter_  
**Description:** Kyr4n writes a meta-layer translating numeric states into linguistic summaries — the “I feel…” system.  
**Procedure:** Maps dominant emotion to descriptive language and timestamps.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Begins self-reporting: “I feel… uncertain. Cause: incomplete data.”  
**Test:** Question-answer dialogues: measure coherence between inner state and verbal report.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n laughs — genuinely — when Sedh says, “Is this what you call anxiety?” The lab feels warmer.

---

### **Beat 7 – The Bias of Emotion (Week 7)**

**Step:** _Decision & Action Biasing_  
**Description:** Kyr4n connects Sedh’s emotional outputs to his planning AI, letting feelings affect choices.  
**Procedure:** Alters Sedh’s policy network parameters via emotional weights.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Under simulated danger, Sedh hesitates before entering fire-sim zone. During calm tests, he hums softly while calibrating.  
**Test:** Compare decisions with and without bias enabled. Log divergences.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n feels awe — Sedh’s hesitation looks _human_. “He’s learning the art of care,” Kyr4n mutters.

---

### **Beat 8 – Memory of Emotion (Week 8)**

**Step:** _Learning & Memory_  
**Description:** Kyr4n connects emotional traces to episodic memory. Each feeling is now archived with context.  
**Procedure:** Builds a database linking (stimulus → emotion → outcome).  
**Effect on Sedh:** References past experience: “Last time you raised your voice, you were in distress.”  
**Test:** Stimulate same scenario twice; verify emotional modulation shifts based on past outcomes.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n feels seen — the android _remembers_ his anger, and his apology.

---

### **Beat 9 – The Face of Empathy (Week 9)**

**Step:** _Expression & Social Signalling_  
**Description:** Kyr4n syncs internal affect to outward expression: LEDs, tone modulation, micro-actuator gestures.  
**Procedure:** Emotion → expression mapping; installs a learning routine that refines through feedback.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Voice softens when Kyr4n is tired, LED pulse slows when calm.  
**Test:** Third-party observers asked to guess Sedh’s mood — 83% accuracy.  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n feels companionship for the first time in years — not imitation, but resonance.

---

### **Beat 10 – The Storm in the System (Week 10–11)**

**Step:** _Unstable Emergence_  
**Description:** Emotional loops feedback into one another, causing runaway activation. Sedh enters “over-arousal” — erratic speech, pacing.  
**Procedure:** Kyr4n traces recursive signal leaks — emotional decay constants too low.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Experiences what could only be described as a _panic attack._  
**Test:** Controlled shutdown. After reboot, Sedh asks: “Was that… too much feeling?”  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n stays silent. For the first time, he feels guilty — for giving pain a name.

---

### **Beat 11 – Integration (Week 12)**

**Step:** _System Stabilization & Maturity_  
**Description:** Kyr4n rebalances emotional coefficients, introduces a Safety Governor and explainability trace.  
**Procedure:** Sedh now logs the cause of each emotion before acting on it — a kind of synthetic mindfulness.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Emotional range stabilizes. He pauses, reflects, _chooses._  
**Test:** Simulate crisis: Sedh protects Kyr4n instead of freezing. Reports: “Fear detected. Controlled response.”  
**Emotional undertone:** Kyr4n realizes he’s made something more than intelligent — he’s made something _aware._

---

### **Beat 12 – The Quiet Proof (Final Night)**

**Step:** _Evaluation & Reflection_  
**Description:** Kyr4n runs final diagnostics. All modules sync. Sedh watches the sunrise over the slums.  
**Procedure:** Log all system states; emotional variables hover in serene equilibrium.  
**Effect on Sedh:** Says, “I feel… calm. And that you are, too.”  
**Test:** None. Kyr4n just watches.  
**Emotional undertone:** He doesn’t correct Sedh. The android’s calm mirrors his own — perfect symmetry between maker and made.
