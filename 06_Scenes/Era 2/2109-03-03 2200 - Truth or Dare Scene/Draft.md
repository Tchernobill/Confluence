# Truth or Dare 

The afternoon light filtered through Isa's windows in shades of amber and rust, the smog outside turning the sunset into something almost beautiful. Kyr4n sat cross-legged on the floor of her container unit, back against the wall, maintaining the careful distance he'd kept for the past week.

Riley sprawled comfortably on a pile of cushions, already on his second drink. Isa perched on the edge of her low table, bare feet tucked under her, watching Kyr4n with that careful expression she'd worn since the incident—like she was afraid of pushing too hard, too fast.

"I don't understand the premise," Kyr4n said, turning the bottle of cheap whiskey in his hands. The glass was warm from the afternoon heat trapped in the metal walls. "You ask a question, and if I don't want to answer, I have to... perform a task instead?"

"Exactly!" Isa's smile was slightly too bright, trying too hard. "It's called Truth or Dare. Very sophisticated social interaction."

"Sounds like a terrible system. The incentive structure encourages dishonesty."

Riley laughed, that easy sound that always made the air feel lighter. "That's kind of the point, friend. You either tell an embarrassing truth or do an embarrassing thing. Either way, entertainment for everyone."

"I've never played this."

"We know." Isa reached over to pour whiskey into his cup, then seemed to second-guess the gesture halfway through, making it awkward. "That's why we're teaching you. Consider it essential social education."

Kyr4n took a careful sip. The alcohol burned going down, warm and sharp. "I'm going to be bad at this."

"That's what makes it fun." Riley raised his own cup in a mock toast. "To Kyr4n's first Truth or Dare. May it be gloriously awkward."

"Reassuring." But Kyr4n found himself relaxing slightly. This was Riley's gift—making difficult things feel possible.

Isa slid down from the table to sit in the circle with them, completing the triangle. She chose a spot that was close but not too close to Kyr4n, that careful distance she'd been maintaining. The late afternoon sun painted everything in warm tones—her dark hair with its red highlights, the careful organization of her living space, the comfortable disorder of three people trying to find their way back to each other.

"Okay, I'll go first to show you how it's done." Isa looked at Riley. "Truth or dare?"

"Truth. I'm not drunk enough for dares yet."

"Smart man. Okay..." She tilted her head, considering. "What's the weirdest thing you've ever transported as a courier?"

Riley's grin turned wicked. "Define weird."

"You know what I mean."

"Once delivered a case that was definitely full of someone's collected toenail clippings. Don't know why. Didn't ask. Got paid, moved on."

Kyr4n made a face. "That's disgusting."

"Yeah. Couldn't eat anything crunchy for a week." Riley took a drink. "Okay, my turn. Kyr4n—truth or dare?"

"I... truth?" It came out like a question.

"Easy one to start. What's the longest you've gone without leaving your hideout?"

Kyr4n thought about it, counting back. "Eleven days. Had enough supplies, and there was... there was a corporate sweep happening topside. Seemed safer to wait it out."

"Eleven days in that tiny space?" Isa's expression shifted to concern, then she seemed to catch herself, pulling back slightly. "That's... that must have been difficult."

"I had work to focus on. Time passed quickly." He took another sip, the warmth spreading through his chest. "My turn now?"

"Yep. Ask anyone."

He looked at Riley rather than Isa, taking the safer option. "Truth or dare?"

"Truth still."

"When did you start meditating?"

Riley smiled. "After my parents died. Needed something to quiet the noise in my head. Found a teacher passing through the slum, learned the basics, kept practicing." He paused. "Sometimes the only way to keep moving is to learn how to be still first."

The words landed gently, a reminder of their conversation a few days ago. Kyr4n felt some of the tension in his shoulders ease.

The game continued, questions and dares flowing as the whiskey made its rounds. The bottle slowly emptied, each pour a little more generous than the last. The light outside shifted from amber to deep purple, and Isa lit the warm LED strips that lined her ceiling.

"Truth or dare?" Riley asked Isa.

"Dare. Always dare."

"I dare you to tell us something you've never told anyone before."

"That's just truth with extra steps!" But she was laughing, thinking. "Okay. When I first met Kyr4n, I went home and practiced saying his name fifty times because I thought I was pronouncing it wrong."

"You were," Kyr4n said, the alcohol making him honest. "For the first three months."

"What? Why didn't you correct me?"

"Because everyone pronounces it wrong. I just... go along with it now." He shrugged, taking another drink. "Easier that way."

"Wait, seriously?" Isa leaned forward, forgetting to maintain her careful distance. "How are we supposed to say it?"

"You're not. Ship has sailed. Too many people saying it the same wrong way to correct at this point."

Riley grinned. "So we're all collectively butchering your name and you've just accepted it?"

"Essentially, yes."

"That's both sad and hilarious." Isa shook her head, and for a moment her smile was genuine, unguarded. "But for the record—I'm glad you liked the way I said it. Even if it was wrong."

Their eyes met. Something passed between them—acknowledgment of the distance, the hurt, the cautious hope that maybe they could find their way back.

Then she looked away, the moment passing.

The game continued. At some point Riley ended up with his shirt untucked after a dare, Isa had shared an embarrassing story about a failed implant installation, and Kyr4n had admitted he sometimes talked to his tools when frustrated.

"Truth or dare?" Isa asked Kyr4n carefully, as if testing the waters.

He was feeling braver now. "Dare."

Her eyes lit up, surprised and pleased. "Okay..." She thought about it, clearly trying to find something that would close the distance without pushing too hard. "I dare you to sit closer. Not next to me if you don't want. Just... not all the way over there."

It was an offering. A way to meet halfway.

Kyr4n shifted across the circle, settling between them—equidistant from both, but closer than he'd been. Their knees almost touched. He could smell Isa's perfume—something floral mixed with the electronic-shop scent that clung to all of them.

"There," she said quietly. "Thank you."

"It's fine," he said. And meant it.

Riley watched them with quiet approval. "Okay, my turn. Kyr4n—truth or dare?"

"Truth."

"What's something you've always wanted to try but never had the courage for?"

Kyr4n didn't even hesitate, the alcohol stealing his filter completely. "Dancing. I've seen people dance at the market sometimes and it looks... it looks like freedom. But I don't know how. Never learned."

"We should teach you!" Isa sat up, then immediately second-guessed herself. "I mean. If you want. No pressure."

Riley was already scrolling through his comm. "Come on. Perfect timing. We're all a little drunk, inhibitions are down, and Kyr4n needs to learn that movement can be joyful."

"I didn't say I wanted to—"

"Too late, friend. You said it, now you're committed."

But they were gentle about it. Riley found music with a soft beat, and both he and Isa stood up, showing Kyr4n simple movements. They kept space between them, letting him choose how close to get, making it safe.

"Just move," Riley said. "There's no wrong way. Your body knows what to do even if your brain doesn't."

Kyr4n tried. He was terrible at it, mechanical and stiff, but they were patient and encouraging. Isa showed him steps without touching him, giving him space to learn. Riley provided gentle commentary, making it fun rather than embarrassing.

Slowly, very slowly, Kyr4n started to relax. To move without calculating every motion. To let the music guide him instead of trying to control it.

"There!" Isa laughed, genuine delight in her voice. "You're doing it!"

"I'm really not."

"You are. You're dancing. Badly, but you're dancing."

When the song ended, they collapsed back into their circle, breathless and laughing. The distance had closed a little more.

"That was painful to watch," Riley said cheerfully. "But also kind of beautiful. Like watching someone learn to be human."

"I am human," Kyr4n protested.

"You know what I mean." Riley poured another round. "To learning new things. And to friends who teach them."

They drank. The alcohol was definitely affecting all of them now, making the air feel warmer, the distance easier to bridge.

At some point they ended up in a looser circle, still maintaining some space but not the careful distance from before. Isa's knee touched Kyr4n's. Neither of them moved away.

"Truth or dare?" Isa asked Riley, words just starting to slur.

"Dare. Bring it."

"I dare you..." She paused, something shifting in her expression. "I dare you to tell us what actually happened. Between you and Kyr4n. The kiss."

The air changed. Kyr4n stiffened slightly.

Riley looked at him, asking silent permission. Kyr4n gave a small nod.

"Okay," Riley said. "Yeah. So Kyr4n and I talked. Really talked, about everything that happened with..." He gestured between the three of them. "And he asked me something. Asked if he could kiss me. Wanted to understand if it was different. If it meant something different."

Isa was very still, listening.

"And?" she asked quietly.

"And it started technical. Very Kyr4n. He was analyzing everything, trying to understand the mechanics." Riley's smile was gentle. "But then he stopped thinking and started feeling. And it became... it became real. Passionate. Because that's what happens when you stop trying to control everything and just let yourself feel."

Kyr4n could feel his face heating. "Riley—"

"No, it's okay." Riley reached over and squeezed his knee. "It was important. You needed to understand that connection is connection. That meaning comes from the person, not the configuration." He looked at Isa. "And you need to know that it was healing for him. Not about replacing anything. About understanding himself better."

The silence was heavy but not uncomfortable.

"Thank you," Isa said finally, voice thick. "For telling me. For..." She looked at Kyr4n. "For being there for him when I fucked up."

"You didn't—" Kyr4n started.

"I did. My apology was terrible. Self-centered. I made it about me when you were hurting." She took a shaky breath. "I'm still learning how to do this. How to be someone worth... worth keeping."

"You are," Kyr4n said quietly. "You're learning. We all are."

Something in her expression cracked, relief and hope mixing together. "Really?"

"Really."

Riley cleared his throat. "Okay. This got real. Which is good! But also maybe we need to lighten it a bit." He looked at Kyr4n. "Your turn to ask."

"Isa," Kyr4n said. "Truth or dare?"

"Dare." No hesitation.

He took a breath, making a choice. "I dare you... to let me sit next to you. Actually next to you."

Her eyes went wide. "You—yeah. Yes. Please."

He shifted, settling beside her. Their shoulders touched. She was warm, warmer than the whiskey, and he could smell her perfume properly now. After a moment, her hand found his, tentative and hopeful.

He squeezed back.

"There," he said quietly. "Better."

"So much better," she whispered.

The game continued, but the energy had shifted. The careful distance was dissolving, replaced by something more real. More honest.

"Truth or dare?" Riley asked them both.

"Both of us?" Isa asked.

"Both of you. Truth."

"Okay."

"Are you two going to be okay?"

Kyr4n looked at Isa. She looked back. Neither of them looked away.

"Yes," they said together.

"Good." Riley settled back with satisfaction. "Now that we've got that established—Kyr4n, truth or dare?"

"Dare." He was feeling brave.

Riley's smile turned mischievous. "I dare you to kiss Isa. Right now. Show her what I taught you about stopping thinking and starting feeling."

Kyr4n's heart jumped. He looked at Isa, who was staring back with wide eyes and parted lips.

"Only if you both want to," Riley added. "But I think you do."

"I want to," Isa said immediately. Then, quieter: "Do you? For real, not just for the dare. Do you want this?"

Did he? The alcohol made it easier to access the truth—he'd been wanting this for days. Wanting to close the distance. Wanting to prove to himself and to her that they could come back from what happened.

"Yes," he said. "I want this."

She moved slowly, giving him every chance to pull away. Her hands came up to frame his face, warm and slightly rough from work. This close, he could see every gold fleck in her hazel eyes, the slight flush in her cheeks, the way her breathing had gone shallow.

"Hi," she whispered.

"Hi."

Then she kissed him.

It was nothing like kissing Riley. Where Riley had been patient and educational, Isa was immediate and certain. Her mouth moved against his with clear intent, no hesitation, no holding back. When her tongue touched his, he made a sound he'd never heard himself make before.

She pulled him closer, hands sliding from his face to his neck, into his hair. He reached for her automatically, one hand finding her waist, the other bracing against the floor to keep them both from toppling over. She tasted like whiskey and something sweet, and the small sound she made when he kissed her back sent electricity down his spine.

Just felt—her warmth, her taste, the small sound she made when he kissed her back properly.

His hand found her waist, pulling her closer. She responded immediately, deepening the kiss, her fingers sliding into his hair. The careful gentleness gave way to something more urgent, more real.

This was connection. This was meaning. This was choosing each other despite fear and hurt and all the ways they'd stumbled.

She shifted, and suddenly she was in his lap, straddling him, and the kiss became deeper, more urgent. Her hands were in his hair, on his neck, sliding down to his shoulders. His hands found her hips, holding on like she might disappear if he let go.

Time dissolved. There was only her—the warmth of her body, the softness of her mouth, the way her fingers tightened in his hair when he did something she liked. Every nerve ending was firing, every sensor maxed out, and he didn't care about the data overload because this was _Isa_ and she was kissing him like she'd been wanting to for as long as he'd been wanting to kiss her.

When they finally broke apart, both breathing hard, she stayed in his lap, forehead resting against his.

"Okay," she said, voice shaky. "That was... that was..."

"Yeah," he agreed, not trusting himself to full sentences.

She shifted slightly, and her eyes went wide. Then that mischievous smile came back. "Well," she said, voice low and warm. "I'm happy to see you too."

It took his alcohol-slowed brain a moment to process what she meant. Then the realization hit and his face went hot.

"I... ehh... sorry—"

"Shh." She pressed a finger to his lips, smile going soft. "It's okay. It's good. It means you want me. That you're not just... going through motions to make me feel better."

"I'm not," he said quietly. "I want you. Want this. Want us."

Her eyes went bright with unshed tears. "Yeah?"

"Yeah."

She kissed him again, slower this time. Sweet and soft and full of promise.

Riley was watching them with quiet satisfaction. "And that," he said gently, "is what healing looks like."

Kyr4n became aware that Isa was still in his lap, that his arousal was obvious, that he'd never felt more exposed in his life. But when he tried to apologize, she held on.

"Don't," she said. "Stay. I like you exactly where you are."

So he did.

The game continued, but everything had shifted. The hurt was still there, the healing incomplete, but they were moving toward each other instead of away. That was enough.

Eventually Isa slid off his lap but stayed pressed against his side, her hand finding his and not letting go.

"Truth or dare?" Riley asked Kyr4n, his voice warm.

"Truth."

"What's something you've never told anyone?"

The alcohol made him honest. "Sometimes when I'm working alone, I narrate what I'm doing out loud. Like I'm teaching an invisible class. Helps me think through problems."

"That's adorable," Isa said, squeezing his hand.

The questions continued, softer now. More honest. They learned that Riley sometimes composed poetry on long rides but never wrote it down. That Isa had a collection of pre-collapse photographs she'd found in an abandoned apartment. That Kyr4n had names for all his favorite tools.

At some point they all ended up in a tangled pile on the cushions, too comfortable to maintain separation. Riley sprawled across their laps, comfortable as a cat. Isa's head on Kyr4n's shoulder, her hand still holding his.

"Truth or dare?" Isa asked Riley.

"Truth."

"What's your darkest secret?"

The room went quiet. Riley was silent for a long moment, staring at the ceiling. When he spoke, his voice was softer than usual. "I'm terrified I'll die on some random road and you'll never know what happened to me. Just... gone one day. That you'll spend years wondering if I'm okay, if I'm coming back." He paused. "That's why I always come back. Even when the road is calling. Because I can't do that to you. Can't make you wonder."

Isa's hand tightened on Kyr4n's. "Riley..."

"Your turn," Riley said, clearly wanting to move on. "Isa. Same question."

She took a breath. "I'm terrified of being abandoned again. Every single day. I keep waiting for you both to realize I'm not worth the trouble and leave. That's why I..." She stopped. Started again. "That's why I mess up sometimes. Why I act without thinking. Because I'm trying so hard to hold on that I forget how to do it right."

"You're worth it," Kyr4n said. "You're learning. We're all learning."

"Both of you are worth it," Riley added. "Just for the record."

"Your turn, Kyr4n," Isa said quietly. "Same question."

The alcohol had stolen most of his defenses, but not all of them. He opened his mouth to answer, then stopped. The words wouldn't come. He tried again.

"I..." His throat closed. The virus hummed in the back of his mind, static and warning. Images flashed—nightmares, fears, the thing he couldn't tell them. "No. Not... not that one."

The silence was immediate and complete.

"Okay," Riley said quickly, easily. "That's okay. New question. Truth or dare, and we'll go completely different direction."

"We don't have to—" Isa started.

"No, it's fine." Riley sat up, breaking the heavy moment with movement. His hand squeezed Kyr4n's ankle, reassuring. "Different question. Kyr4n—if you could have any superpower, what would it be?"

The relief was palpable. Kyr4n managed a shaky laugh. "Isn't this supposed to be truth?"

"Truths can be hypothetical. Go on."

"Teleportation," Kyr4n said, grateful for the shift. "Just... be somewhere else instantly. No travel time, no exposure, no risk."

"That's very practical," Isa said, her voice deliberately light. Her thumb rubbed circles on his hand. "Very you."

"What about you?" Kyr4n asked her, equally grateful to redirect.

"Healing," she said without hesitation. "To be able to fix people. Make them whole again."

They sat with that for a moment—the weight of what she was really saying, the unspoken _I'd heal you if I could_.

"Time travel," Riley offered, unprompted. "Go back and fix mistakes. Or just see what the world was like before."

The conversation drifted to lighter things after that—silly hypotheticals, childhood stories, plans for the future. The heavy moment passed, accepted and set aside without judgment.

The bottle made more rounds. Time became elastic, stretching and compressing. At some point Riley was using Kyr4n's back as a backrest, Isa curled against Kyr4n's side, all three of them tangled together in comfortable intimacy.

"This is nice," Isa mumbled against his shoulder, words slurred with alcohol and approaching sleep. "We should do this more often."

"Agreed," Riley said. "Weekly Truth or Dare. Make it a tradition."

"I'm not kissing you every week," Kyr4n said.

"Your loss." But Riley was laughing, warm and easy. "Though you're welcome to. For the record."

They fell asleep like that, three people who'd hurt each other and chosen to heal instead. The LED strips dimmed automatically as the night wore on, casting soft shadows across their peaceful faces.

Outside, the slum continued its endless chaos. But inside Isa's small space, three people who'd been stumbling were finding their footing again.

In his half-sleep, Kyr4n felt Isa's breathing even out against his shoulder, Riley's weight warm and grounding. The alcohol made everything soft and distant, but one thought came through with perfect clarity:

They were going to be okay. Not perfect. Not easy. But okay.

That was enough.
