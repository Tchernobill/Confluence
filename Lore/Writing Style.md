# Confluence Novel Style Guide

## Genre Classification

**Semi-Cyberpunk with Slice-of-Life Elements**

- Blends traditional cyberpunk aesthetics with contemplative, everyday moments
- Technology exists as backdrop rather than driving force
- Focus on human experience within technological landscape

## Core Narrative Principles

### Character-Driven Storytelling

- **Priority**: Character struggles and interactions drive plot progression
- **Focus Areas**: Mental health, personal relationships, isolation, personal growth
- **Avoid**: Traditional hero's journey arcs
- **Technique**: Let character conversations and emotional journeys reveal story developments

### Atmospheric Worldbuilding

- **Method**: "Show don't tell" through environmental details
- **Elements**: Building descriptions, character observations, everyday experiences
- **Goal**: Organic immersion without exposition dumps
- **Technique**: Layer world details through protagonist's daily interactions

### Slow-Burning Narrative Structure

- **Timeline**: Story spans decades with gradual evolution
- **Pacing**: Contemplative and observational
- **Content**: Include mundane activities and character interactions without major plot beats
- **Purpose**: Allow nuanced characterization and deep theme exploration

### Tonal Balance

- **Primary Tone**: Gritty cyberpunk atmosphere
- **Counterbalance**: Dry wit, absurdity, gentle humor
- **Goal**: Prevent oversomberness while addressing serious topics
- **Technique**: Whimsical moments to lighten darker themes

## Narrative Structure

### Arc-Based Organization

- Each major section follows different protagonist
- Main protagonist appears across multiple arcs as evolving, mysterious figure
- Arc protagonists intersect with main protagonist during specific life phases
- Main character's true nature revealed gradually through multiple perspectives

### Point of View Guidelines

- **Style**: Third person limited
- **Alternation**: Between main protagonist and current arc protagonist
- **Technique**: Internal dialogue and thoughts for character insights
- **Consistency**: Maintain distinct voice for each POV character

## Thematic Inspirations & Applications

### Technology & Humanity Interface

**From Ghost in the Shell**

- Consciousness and identity questions when memories can be artificial
- Body/identity fluidity and augmentation philosophy
- "What makes us real?" when everything is replaceable
- Apply to character awakening moments and self-discovery arcs

### Corporate Dystopia Elements

**From Strange Days & Demolition Man**

- Class divide through technology access
- Clean corporate zones vs. messy underground reality
- Technology addiction and dependency themes
- Social control through comfort rather than fear

### Enhancement & Consequences

**From Johnny Mnemonic & Cyberpunk Edgerunners**

- Neural enhancement costs (physical/mental prices)
- Technology pushing beyond safe limits
- Street-level innovation vs. corporate research
- Cyberpsychosis: losing humanity through augmentation

### Underground Culture Dynamics

**From Multiple Sources**

- Found family dynamics within hostile world
- Choosing dangerous freedom over safe oppression
- Old skills becoming valuable in high-tech environments
- Information as ultimate commodity

### Strategic Complexity

**From Eminence in Shadow**

- Misunderstanding between actions and interpretations
- Long-term strategic planning and shadow influence
- Competence through preparation and skill
- Reluctant leadership and power accumulation

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Atmospheric Description

- Emphasize neon-lit urban decay
- Corporate dystopia through environmental details
- Human-tech intersection in everyday scenes
- Lived-in, authentic rather than sterile environments

### Dialogue Approach

- Character-specific voices and speech patterns
- Conversations reveal world and character depth
- Avoid exposition through dialogue
- Include subtext and underlying tensions

### Pacing Techniques

- Alternate between action and contemplative moments
- Use mundane activities to develop character
- Build tension through character interactions
- Allow scenes to breathe without forcing plot advancement

### Humor Integration

- Dry wit during serious moments
- Absurdity in everyday cyberpunk situations
- Gentle humor to counterbalance darker themes
- Character-appropriate comedic moments

## Consistency Checkpoints

### Character Development

- Track emotional arcs across multiple time periods
- Maintain voice consistency for each POV character
- Ensure character growth feels organic over decades
- Balance mystery revelation with character agency

### World Continuity

- Technology level consistency across timeline
- Corporate and social structure evolution
- Geographic and cultural details
- Economic and political backdrop maintenance

### Tone Management

- Balance cyberpunk grittiness with slice-of-life warmth
- Maintain whimsical elements without undermining serious themes
- Ensure humor feels natural, not forced
- Keep contemplative pacing without becoming static

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Over-explaining technology or world mechanics
- Rushed character development or relationship changes
- Losing slice-of-life feel for action sequences
- Making main protagonist too mysterious or too revealed too quickly
- Forcing humor that doesn't fit character or situation
