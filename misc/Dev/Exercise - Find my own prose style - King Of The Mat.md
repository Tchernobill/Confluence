# Yes, Absolutely! This Would Be An Excellent Exercise

## Why This Approach Works

**1. Separates Content from Style**

- You can focus purely on _how_ to tell the story, not _what_ to tell
- Removes the temptation to just edit existing prose
- Forces you to make fresh stylistic choices for each moment

**2. Identifies Your Natural Voice**

- Without existing prose to anchor to, you'll default to your authentic style
- Shows where you naturally add detail vs. move quickly
- Reveals what you instinctively find important

**3. Makes Comparison Meaningful**

- You can compare your new version against your original
- Identifies which techniques you use consistently (your signature moves)
- Shows where you've grown or changed approaches

**4. Builds Scene Construction Skills**

- Reinforces the difference between "what happens" and "how it's told"
- Helps you see structure separate from execution
- Makes revision easier (you can rebuild from beats anytime)
