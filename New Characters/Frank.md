## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Franklin "Frank" Carver  
**Nickname/Alias:** Frank (exclusively), "The Architect" (professional reputation)  
**Age (Current/Tome Relevant):**

- Tome 2 (2108): 34 years old
- Tome 3 (2115): 41 years old

**Born:** 2074, South Shore Slum, Neo-Montreal  
**Current Residence:** Rotates between safe houses across South Shore and Mid-City zones  
**Occupation:** Professional contractor/heist specialist, crew leader  
**Social Class:** Mid-level criminal operator—successful enough for comfort, not successful enough to escape the game

- **Tome 2:** Recruiter and heist leader whose panic destroys Kyr4n's life

## Background & History

### Early Life (2074-2092, Ages 0-18)

- Born to struggling Slum family, father was failed criminal who drank himself to death
- Mother worked triple shifts in Mid-City service sector, rarely home
- Raised partially by older sister who died in corporate security action at 19
- Smart kid who realized charm and intelligence were survival tools
- Ran minor scams and errands for local criminals as teenager
- Learned that planning beats improvisation, but witnessed too much violence too young

### Career Development (2092-2108, Ages 18-34)

- Started as crew member for various contractors
- Survived by being smart, likeable, and knowing when to run
- Built reputation as excellent planner with gift for recruiting right specialists
- Established "The Architect" reputation for solid, professional operations
- Lost crew members to panicked mistakes early career—learned wrong lessons about aggression
- Successfully led dozens of operations before the Corporate Facility Heist disaster
- Maintained clean professional image: intelligent contractor, not violent thug

### The Heist That Changed Everything (2108-2109, Age 34)

**Before the disaster:**

- Spotted Egypt NPU opportunity through intelligence network
- Needed technical specialist for advanced security systems
- Researched Kyr4n through underground networks—genius reputation
- Recruited with genuine professional respect and smart pitch (Egypt NPUs for Sedh)
- Planned meticulous operation, everything checked and double-checked
- Actually liked Kyr4n during planning—respected his intelligence, enjoyed the collaboration

**During the disaster:**

- Crew member's negligence triggers security (unnamed member, not Frank's direct fault)
- Emergency lockdown activates—operation becomes survival scenario
- Frank's intelligence abandons him—defaults to "shoot our way out" panic response
- Situation escalates to grenades and explosions in R&D lab
- Grenade blast near Kyr4n—breaches containment, viral exposure (Frank doesn't know this detail)
- Kyr4n down, injured, potentially dying
- Frank's survival instinct: leaves DeathPack explosive to cover escape, abandons Kyr4n
- Tank (crew member) disobeys orders, rescues Kyr4n against Frank's directive
- Frank escapes, operation technically succeeds (obtained primary objective)

**After the disaster:**

- Guilt hits immediately—abandoned someone who trusted him, potentially left him to die
- Pays substantial guilt bonus to Kyr4n beyond agreed compensation
- Doesn't visit or check on Kyr4n—too ashamed, too cowardly
- Hears through network that Kyr4n survived but "something happened"
- Continues operations but plagued by nightmares about the lab explosion
- Crew dynamics shift—Tank's disobedience created crack in authority
- Professional reputation intact (survivors don't advertise failures), personal conscience destroyed

### Years Between Tomes (2109-2115, Ages 35-41)

- Continued contractor work but more cautious, fewer high-risk operations
- Built wider intelligence network—knowledge is safety
- Maintained distance from Phoenix Recovery and Kyr4n
- Accumulated resources and contacts but couldn't fully enjoy success
- Started hearing rumors about "The Ghost" in South Shore—didn't connect to Kyr4n
- Flux joins his crew, becomes infected with DarkNight virus
- Exhausts options for treatment, finally admits he has to face Kyr4n

## Psychology & Internal Life

### Core Beliefs

- "Intelligence and planning beat raw force—except when everything goes wrong, then you shoot your way out"
- "Loyalty is transactional—everyone's motivated by self-interest, including me"
- "The smart survive, the proud die young"
- "Some mistakes can't be unmade, only lived with"
- "Success is about managing risk, but sometimes risk manages you"

### Fears & Desires

- **Deepest Fear:** Being trapped with no escape, operations going catastrophically wrong, facing consequences for the Kyr4n betrayal
- **Greatest Desire:** Success and security without the guilt, redemption that can never actually happen
- **Secret Want:** To go back to the heist and make different choices, to prove he's not fundamentally a coward

### Moral Framework

- **Ethical Code:** Professional criminals can have honor—but survival always comes first

- **Justifications:** "I panicked," "Tank saved him anyway," "I paid him extra," "He survived"
- **Guilt Management:** Compartmentalizes betrayal, stays busy, tells himself it was survival

### Mental Patterns

- **Problem-Solving:** Strategic analysis, multiple contingencies, backup plans for backup plans
- **Under Stress:** Intelligence degrades rapidly, defaults to aggressive action, survival override
- **Learning Style:** Observational, analysis of past operations (both successes and failures)
- **Decision Process:** Logical risk-benefit when calm; pure panic survival when threatened
- **Internal Monologue:** Constant strategic assessment, guilt intrusions about Kyr4n, dark humor to cope

## Writing Reminders

### Character Consistency

- **Remember:** Frank is intelligent and charismatic UNTIL extreme pressure, then becomes desperate survivor
- **Show:** Charm and strategic thinking in normal operations, progressive degradation under stress
- **Contrast:** Professional competence with panic response, genuine guilt with self-preservation instinct

### Avoiding Pitfalls

- **Don't:** Make him purely sympathetic—the betrayal was unforgivable
- **Don't:** Make him pure villain—he's human with fatal flaws, not evil
- **Don't:** Let charm excuse consequences—intelligence doesn't absolve cowardice
- **Do:** Show complexity—capable of genuine remorse while being fundamentally self-serving

### Scene Writing Tips

- **In Calm Scenes:** Show intelligence, charm, strategic thinking—make readers understand why people work with him
- **In Stress Scenes:** Show progressive degradation—smart decisions becoming desperate ones
- **In Guilty Scenes:** Show self-awareness without self-forgiveness—he knows what he is
- **With Kyr4n:** Show wariness, guilt, carefully maintained professional distance covering massive tension

### Voice Markers

- Uses "Listen..." when making important points
- Strategic profanity—never crude, always purposeful
- Pop culture references and dark humor to build rapport
- Shorter sentences and fragments when stressed
- More formal, over-explained language when lying or guilty
- "The Architect" professional pride contrasts with personal shame

## Final Notes

Frank is **not a villain**—he's a professionally competent person who revealed catastrophic character failure under the worst possible circumstances. He's smart enough to know he can never fix what he broke, self-aware enough to be haunted by it, but still fundamentally prioritizes his own survival.

The Simon Phoenix inspiration provides charisma and intelligence that makes his failure more tragic—he _should_ have been better, which makes his panic response more devastating. He's the kind of person you'd enjoy working with right up until the moment everything falls apart and his true nature emerges.

He lives in the uncomfortable space between redemption and damnation—too self-aware to excuse himself, too cowardly to truly make amends, too useful to completely cut off, too guilty to ever be comfortable.

His relationship with Kyr4n is permanently broken but professionally necessary—a constant reminder that some mistakes can't be unmade, only managed.
