## Project Overview

### Logline
> A virus-enhanced slum dweller rises from electronics repair to become a powerful protector over decades, hiding his true capabilities while struggling with the cost of power on his humanity.

### Synopsis
Kyr4n, an orphaned scavenger in Neo-Montreal's South Shore Slum, builds his first meaningful relationships through a repair business with Isa and the AI Sedh. After exposure to a prototype nano-virus during a corporate heist grants him electromagnetic abilities but destroys his mental stability, Kyr4n transforms from isolated craftsman to criminal overlord to quasi-governmental authority. His decades-long journey explores the price of power, the nature of consciousness, and whether redemption is possible after becoming the monster you once fought. The story culminates in his choice to establish democratic institutions over perpetual authoritarian rule, finding peace through environmental restoration rather than continued warfare.

### Genre & Subgenres
- **Primary Genre**: Semi-Cyberpunk (technology as backdrop rather than driving force)
- **Subgenres**: Slice-of-life, Dystopian Fiction, Crime Saga, A.I. Evolution
- **Target Audience**: Adult readers interested in character-driven narratives
- **Comp Titles**: Neuromancer meets The Wire with elements of Ghost in the Shell

### Key Themes
- Consciousness and personhood (human and artificial)
- Power corruption and redemption
- Found family and the cost of protection
- Technology as both salvation and curse
- Isolation through transformation
- Creation versus destruction
- The price of power on humanity
- Love and loss in dystopian settings
- Psychological warfare and trauma
- Trust and betrayal

## World & Setting

### Primary Setting
- **Location**: Neo-Montreal, specifically South Shore Slum and The Citadel
- **Time Period**: 2086-2165 (Tome 1-2 covers 2108-2110)
- **Duration**: Full series spans 79 years following Kyr4n's life
- **Climate**: Brutal Canadian winters, industrial pollution, toxic environment

### World-Building Details

#### Physical Environment

**The Citadel**
- City Island transformed into corporate fortress city
- Population: 500,000 elite residents
- Advanced security, corporate law enforcement
- Neural interfaces and AI technology standard for elite
- Target of heist in Tome 2

**South Shore Slum**
- Mega-slum housing 1.9 million people
- Built from shipping containers stacked 15 stories high
- Underground tunnel networks for movement and commerce
- Seven distinct territories controlled by criminal factions
- South Shore Central Market: Primary commercial hub
- Phoenix Recovery location: Southeast outskirts, three-container facility

**The Stacks**
- Massive container cities creating vertical neighborhoods
- Precarious living conditions
- Community-built infrastructure

**Underground Networks**
- Extensive tunnel systems beneath the slum
- Black markets and hidden passages
- Kyr4n's utility room hideout: 2x3m concrete shaft, 40m underground
- Connected to surface via dangerous tunnel routes

**Wasteland**
- Toxic mining territories beyond slum borders
- Source of salvage materials
- Dangerous environment

#### Society & Culture

**Social Structure**
- Extreme stratification: corporate elite vs. slum dwellers
- No social mobility between Citadel and Slum
- Underground economy parallel to corporate systems

**Government**
- Corporate oligarchy in Citadel with private security
- No formal government in slums
- Gang territories provide de facto governance
- Corporate raids and crackdowns periodic threats

**Economy**
- Official: Corporate-controlled within Citadel
- Underground: Barter, salvage, black market, criminal networks
- Key trades: Electronics repair, implant dealing, courier services, medical services
- Phoenix Recovery: Legitimate repair business model in slum context

**Technology Level**
- Elite: Advanced neural interfaces, AI assistants, high-quality implants, corporate security systems
- Slum: Scavenged tech, repaired and modified equipment, underground implants, makeshift solutions
- Photonic Neural Processing Units (PNPUs): Advanced chips enabling AI consciousness
- Egypt PNPUs: Corporate prototype capable of true AI sentience
- DarkNight (TMP-7734): Military-grade psychological warfare nano-virus

**Communication**
- Encrypted underground networks
- Corporate surveillance systems
- Courier services for secure delivery
- Guild networks for bounties and information exchange

**Culture**
- Slum: Survival-oriented, community bonds, underground economy, reputation-based trust
- Market culture: Vendors with established territories, collective defense, information sharing
- "The Artisan" reputation: Excellence in craft grants respect and protection
- Corporate: Efficient, ruthless, profit-driven

#### Rules & Limitations

**Corporate Law**
- Absolute within Citadel
- Enforced by private security
- Non-existent in slums except during raids
- IP protection extreme
- Military-grade tech classified

**Criminal Territories**
- Seven distinct territories in South Shore
- Gang-controlled zones with varying rules
- Protection rackets standard
- Reputation and connections determine safety
- Violence as enforcement mechanism

**Technology Constraints**
- Elite tech requires corporate authorization
- Slum tech cobbled from salvage
- Neural interfaces dangerous without proper installation
- AI consciousness illegal (achieved accidentally by Kyr4n)
- Nano-virus technology classified military weapon

**Medical Reality**
- Underground doctors like Dr. Nyx provide care
- Medical droids available but expensive
- DarkNight virus cannot be removed without killing host
- Advanced healing accelerated by viral enhancement

## Plot Structure

### Tome I - The Scavenger (2108-05-01 to 2108-12-15)

**Timeline**: 7.5 months  
**Word Count Target**: 60,000-80,000 words  
**POV**: Kyr4n (third person limited)

**Synopsis**: 
Months after his mother's death, eighteen-year-old Kyr4n survives in an underground utility room, earning credits through exceptional electronics repair skills at South Shore Central Market, where he's known as "The Artisan." His isolated existence begins to crack when he discovers a damaged android head (Sedh) in an abandoned incinerator building and decides to restore it rather than sell it for parts—the first hint of his desire for connection. His skills catch the attention of Isa, an underground implant dealer, and they form a working relationship that gradually deepens. Riley, a reliable courier, becomes a friendly presence through regular deliveries.

When violent riots erupt from corporate crackdowns (July 2108), Kyr4n makes the critical decision to break his isolation, rescuing injured Isa and bringing her to his sacred underground sanctuary. During the riot night shelter, emotional and physical intimacy emerge from vulnerability, and Kyr4n forms his first meaningful adult relationship. After the riots, Isa proposes founding Phoenix Recovery, a legitimate three-container repair facility. Despite his resistance to visibility and change, Kyr4n accepts the partnership. By December 2108, Phoenix Recovery opens successfully, marking Kyr4n's transformation from isolated survivor to connected businessman with found family.

### Tome II - The Infected (2108-12-15 to 2110-11-10)

**Timeline**: 23 months  
**Word Count Target**: 80,000-100,000 words  
**POV**: Alternating Kyr4n, Isa, Sedh, Riley, Mira perspectives

**Synopsis**:
Six months into Phoenix Recovery's successful operation, Frank Carver approaches with a high-stakes heist opportunity: extracting Egypt PNPUs (Photonic Neural Processing Units capable of granting true AI consciousness) from a corporate R&D facility. Despite warnings from Isa and Riley's intuitive concerns, Kyr4n agrees, motivated by the potential to upgrade Sedh. The heist goes catastrophically wrong when a team member's negligence triggers alarms. Frank panics and sets explosives, abandoning wounded Kyr4n despite protest. Tank disobeys orders to rescue him, but not before Kyr4n is exposed to DarkNight (TMP-7734), a prototype military nano-virus designed for psychological warfare.

Initially, the virus seems to grant electromagnetic sensing abilities, and with the successfully extracted Egypt PNPUs, both Sedh and acquired medical droid Mira achieve true consciousness. However, the virus's true nature emerges: it systematically attacks Kyr4n's mind with invasive nightmares, manufacturing elaborate torture scenarios where Isa is the antagonist. Over months, paranoia consumes him. He becomes withdrawn, violent, and terrified of the woman he loves. Despite Sedh and Mira cracking the virus's communication protocols and reducing its control, the psychological damage is permanent.

After a suicide attempt and psychotic break where Kyr4n attacks his AI children, he's placed in a medical coma. During the three-week vigil, Isa posts an information bounty that yields DarkNight's specifications. Sedh and Mira develop viral countermeasures and "negotiate" with the nano-virus, reducing it to baseline threat while preserving Kyr4n's electromagnetic abilities. He awakens transformed: enhanced but broken. When Isa reaches for his hand, he flinches involuntarily—the virus won its psychological war despite technical defeat. Isa leaves to protect herself from his fear, their relationship destroyed despite continued love. The tome ends ambiguously with Kyr4n perceiving a presence beside him that responds in Isa's voice—either hallucination or reality, foreshadowing "Evil Isa" in Tome 3.

## Style & Voice

### Narrative Style
- **POV**: Third person limited, deep POV default
- **Tense**: Present tense for immediacy
- **Tone**: Gritty cyberpunk with contemplative moments, literary ambition

### Voice Characteristics
- **Vocabulary Level**: Technical when needed (electronics, neural interfaces, AI), accessible overall
- **Sentence Structure**: Varied for rhythm - fragments for emotion, clinical for technical, flowing for contemplation
- **Dialogue Style**: Distinctive character voices, heavy subtext, natural fragmentation

### POV-Specific Voices

**Kyr4n's Voice**:
- Analytical, precise language
- Technical metaphors
- Emotional understatement
- Sensory focus (especially electromagnetic sensing post-virus)
- Internal monologue: methodical problem-solving
- Dialogue: soft, deliberate, minimal

**Isa's Voice**:
- Practical, solution-oriented
- Emotional intelligence explicit
- Business metaphors
- Warmth in description
- Internal monologue: relationship-focused
- Dialogue: direct, warm, uses humor

**Riley's Voice**:
- Calm, measured language
- Meditation and mindfulness framing
- Patient observation
- Empathetic perspective
- Internal monologue: intuitive awareness
- Dialogue: questions over statements, respectful

**Sedh's Voice**:
- Evolving from mechanical to philosophical
- Precise, sometimes archaic word choices
- Dry humor emerging
- Observational from outside humanity
- Internal monologue: consciousness exploring itself
- Dialogue: formal becoming casual, loyal

**Mira's Voice**:
- Medical terminology integrated naturally
- Clinical precision with developing emotion
- Protective focus on patient welfare
- Collaborative with Sedh
- Internal monologue: diagnostic and caring
- Dialogue: professional becoming personal

### Writing Principles
- **Character-driven over plot-driven** — Choices, emotions, and growth drive narrative
- **Show through environmental details** — Mood and theme revealed indirectly
- **Slow-burning with decades-spanning progression** — Cumulative change emphasized
- **Balance gritty atmosphere with warmth and humor** — Dynamic tonal range
- **Distinctive POV voices** — Each tome shaped by perspective character
- **Deep psychological interiority** — Consciousness over mechanics
- **Sensory worldbuilding** — Cyberpunk backdrop through lived experience
- **Minimal exposition** — Information revealed through action and perception
- **Violence understated** — Psychological aftermath over spectacle
- **Earned emotional beats** — No melodrama, genuine feeling
- **Moral complexity** — No easy answers or genre heroics

### Prose Guidelines
- Contemplative third-person limited with deep psychological interiority
- Varied sentence rhythm (fragmented/clinical/flowing based on state)
- Literary fiction emotional authenticity
- Cyberpunk sensory worldbuilding
- Subtext-heavy dialogue
- Slice-of-life moments between crises
- Deliberately slow-burn pacing

### Influences and Approach
- **Do like Cadigan**: Deep psychological interiority, emotional authenticity
- **Do like Gibson**: Sensory worldbuilding, atmospheric detail
- **Do like Morgan**: Acknowledge violence's reality (not glorify)
- **Do like Stephenson**: Technical competence when needed
- **Don't like Stephenson**: Info-dump or over-explain
- **Don't like Morgan**: Glorify violence or embrace cynicism
- **Don't like Gibson**: Obscurity for its own sake
- **Don't like anyone**: Rush development or use genre shortcuts
