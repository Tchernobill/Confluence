# Prose Style: Understanding the Foundation of Your Novel's Voice

## What Is Prose Style?

Prose style is **how you tell your story** — the distinctive way you construct sentences, choose words, and create rhythm on the page. It's the difference between:

> "He was angry" (telling)

versus

> "His jaw tightened, fingers curling into fists" (showing through sensory detail)

versus

> "Rage bloomed in his chest like fire catching dry tinder" (literary/metaphorical)

versus

> "The virus hummed in his veins, responding to his fury" (your cyberpunk world-specific style)

## Core Elements of Prose Style

### 1. **Sentence Structure**
- **Short, punchy sentences** = urgency, action, emotional intensity
- **Longer, flowing sentences** = contemplation, description, complexity
- **Fragmented sentences** = trauma, dissociation, overwhelming emotion
- **Varied rhythm** = keeps readers engaged

### 2. **Word Choice (Diction)**
- **Simple/direct** vs. **complex/literary**
- **Concrete** (what can be sensed) vs. **abstract** (concepts, emotions)
- **Genre-specific vocabulary** (your cyberpunk terminology)
- **Character-specific language** in dialogue and internal thoughts

### 3. **Point of View & Voice**
- **First person** = intimate, subjective, limited knowledge
- **Third limited** = close to character but with some distance
- **Third omniscient** = broader perspective, multiple viewpoints
- **Voice consistency** = maintaining the same narrative "feel"

### 4. **Sensory Detail & Imagery**
- Which senses you prioritize (sight, sound, touch, etc.)
- How much description vs. action
- Use of metaphor and simile
- Environmental details that establish mood

### 5. **Pacing & Flow**
- Scene vs. summary
- How quickly information is revealed
- Balance of dialogue, action, and introspection

- Action and dialogue revealing character rather than exposition

- Environmental details establishing atmosphere
- Subtext in conversations (what's *not* said matters)

✅ *Understated*: "She turned away. He didn't stop her. The door clicked shut with the finality of every choice he'd made."

## Red Flags for Your Style

Watch out for:

❌ **Over-explaining emotions**: "He felt sad because his past trauma..."
✅ **Showing through behavior**: "He kept three feet between them. Always."

❌ **Info-dumping world-building**: Long paragraphs explaining technology
✅ **Organic integration**: Technology experienced in action

❌ **Melodramatic internal monologue**: "Why, cruel world, why?"
✅ **Thought fragments**: "Safer alone. Safer. Lies. All lies."

❌ **Purple prose in action**: Flowery metaphors during fight scenes
✅ **Clear, visceral description**: Short sentences, active verbs

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

When drafting any scene for *Confluence*:

1. **Whose POV?** How does their perspective filter the description?
2. **What's the emotional temperature?** Should prose be clinical or intense?
3. **Is this action or contemplation?** Adjust sentence rhythm accordingly
4. **What sensory details matter here?** (Especially electromagnetic for Kyr4n)
5. **What's the subtext?** What are characters *not* saying?
6. **Does this match established voice?** Check against earlier scenes
