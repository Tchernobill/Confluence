# Simon Phoenix Character Sheet

## Basic Information

- **Name**: Simon Phoenix
- **Portrayed by**: Wesley Snipes
- **Film**: Demolition Man (1993)
- **Role**: Main Antagonist

## Appearance

Simon Phoenix is depicted as an athletic, muscular man with dark skin. He sports distinctive blonde-dyed hair styled in a high-top fade with shaved sides, often featuring patterns or colors. He wears eclectic, makeshift clothing like denim overalls, bandoliers with ammunition, and accessories such as earrings, giving him a punk, chaotic look that contrasts with the sterile future society.

## Background

In 1996, Simon Phoenix is a notorious criminal and gang leader in Los Angeles, known as the most dangerous felon of the 20th century. He kidnaps a busload of hostages and holds them in an abandoned building rigged with explosives. During a confrontation with LAPD Sergeant John Spartan, the building explodes, killing the hostages. Phoenix frames Spartan for their deaths, leading to both being sentenced to cryogenic freezing in the California Cryo-Penitentiary, where they undergo subliminal rehabilitation.

## Personality

Phoenix is psychopathic, violent, and uncontrollable, thriving on chaos and mayhem. He is taunting and manipulative, often mocking his opponents and society. In the peaceful, vice-free world of 2032, he stands out as an anomaly, delighting in reintroducing violence and disorder. He shows annoyance at non-violent responses and indifference to killing, with a cunning side that allows him to exploit situations and people.

## Abilities and Skills

- **Combat Proficiency**: Expert in hand-to-hand combat, firearms, and improvised weapons. He easily dispatches police officers and engages in intense fights.
- **Hacking and Technology**: Enhanced during cryo-rehabilitation with advanced computer skills, allowing him to hack modern networks effortlessly.
- **Leadership**: Commands loyalty from his gang and thawed cryo-prisoners, leading them in assaults.
- **Physical Enhancements**: Implied superhuman strength and agility from rehabilitation programming, making him a formidable opponent in a society unused to violence.

## Role in the Plot

Thawed in 2032 for a parole hearing, Phoenix escapes after being secretly programmed by Dr. Raymond Cocteau to assassinate resistance leader Edgar Friendly and disrupt the underground Scraps. His rampage exposes the fragility of the utopian San Angeles society. He thaws fellow criminals to form a new gang, clashes with the revived John Spartan, and ultimately meets his end when Spartan freezes his head and causes an explosion in the cryo-prison.

# John Spartan Character Sheet

## Background

In 1996, John Spartan is an LAPD Sergeant known as "The Demolition Man" due to his tendency to cause significant collateral damage while capturing criminals. He leads an unauthorized raid on a building where Simon Phoenix holds hostages, resulting in an explosion that kills the hostages. Phoenix accuses Spartan of knowing about them, leading to both being convicted and sentenced to cryogenic freezing in the California Cryo-Penitentiary, with subliminal rehabilitation programming. While frozen, Spartan is programmed with skills like sewing, which he later uses unexpectedly.

## Personality

Spartan is risk-taking, feisty, and energetic, often acting on instinct rather than protocol. He struggles to adapt to the sterile, overly polite society of 2032 San Angeles, rejecting its restrictions on swearing, violence, and unhealthy behaviors. He values freedom and independence, showing a heroic yet rebellious nature, and forms bonds with those who share his views, like Lenina Huxley and the Scraps.

## Abilities and Skills

- **Combat Expertise**: Highly skilled in hand-to-hand fighting, firearms, and tactical assaults, capable of taking down multiple opponents and handling high-stakes situations.
- **Adaptability**: Quickly learns to navigate future technology and society, using old-school methods effectively against modern threats.
- **Unconventional Skills**: From cryo-rehabilitation, he gains abilities like knitting and sewing, which he employs in creative ways, such as repairing wounds.
- **Physical Prowess**: Exceptional strength and endurance, allowing him to defeat enhanced criminals like Phoenix in direct confrontations.

## Role in the Plot

Thawed in 2032 after Simon Phoenix escapes, Spartan is recruited by the San Angeles Police Department to recapture him, as the peaceful society lacks experience with violent crime. Partnering with Lieutenant Lenina Huxley, he uncovers Dr. Cocteau's conspiracy involving Phoenix's programming to assassinate resistance leader Edgar Friendly. Spartan thwarts the plot, allies with the underground Scraps, and ultimately defeats Phoenix by freezing his head and causing an explosion in the cryo-prison. He advocates for a balanced society and begins a romance with Huxley.

# Owen Harper Character Sheet

## Basic Information

- **Name**: Owen Harper
- **Portrayed by**: Burn Gorman
- **Series**: Torchwood (2006-2011)
- **Role**: Medical Officer and Field Agent

## Appearance

Owen Harper is portrayed as a tall, lean man with short dark hair and a sharp, angular face. He often wears casual professional attire such as button-up shirts, jackets, and trousers, reflecting his medical background while fitting into the team's fieldwork. His expressions frequently convey sarcasm or intensity.

## Background

Born on February 14, 1980 (or possibly 1981) in London's East End, Owen had a troubled upbringing with a distant mother and lost his father to an aortic aneurysm, motivating him to pursue medicine. He trained for seven years, earning a BMBS and MD, and worked as a junior doctor in Cardiff. His fianc√©e, Katie Russell, died from an alien parasite mistaken for a brain tumor, leading to his recruitment by Captain Jack Harkness into Torchwood Three after Owen's determination impressed him. Prior relationships included a brief engagement with medical student Megan Tegg and friendships like with Amira Hussein.

## Personality

Owen is sarcastic, narcissistic, and a habitual womanizer, often using wit to mask deeper torment and empathy. He projects indifference but shows loyalty to his team, protective instincts, and emotional depth, such as real love for a time-lost pilot Diane and regret over unrequited feelings with Toshiko Sato. Post-resurrection as undead, he battles depression and existential despair, yet remains dedicated.

## Abilities and Skills

- **Medical Expertise**: Skilled in treating injuries, poisons, alien conditions, diagnostics, and using advanced tech like reanimating bodies or analyzing chemicals.
- **Combat Proficiency**: Adept with firearms, hand-to-hand fighting, and disarming opponents; effective in field operations.
- **Undead State Enhancements**: After resurrection, he doesn't need to breathe, feels no pain, has no body heat (evading detection), and is ignored or worshipped by aliens like Weevils; however, he can't heal, eat, or engage in physical pleasures, requiring exercise to prevent rigor mortis.
- **Other Skills**: Uses alien devices for mind-reading or influence; quick thinker in crises.

## Role in the Plot

Owen joins Torchwood as its medical officer, participating in alien hunts and investigations. He has affairs with colleagues Gwen and Suzie, experiences a profound romance with Diane that ends tragically, and avenges a murder via an alien device. He temporarily leads the team, opens the Rift causing chaos, and is fired then reinstated. Shot dead by Aaron Copley, he's resurrected undead via a gauntlet, continuing duties despite limitations. In the Series 2 finale, he sacrifices himself in a nuclear meltdown at Turnmill, dying alongside Toshiko after expressing regrets. His legacy influences the team, with mentions in later episodes and spin-offs.
