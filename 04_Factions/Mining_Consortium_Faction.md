# Mining Consortium

> *"Pragmatic workers tired of exploitation—they dig what the Shore needs and want someone who'll treat them fairly."*

---

## Basic Information

### Identity
- **Official Name:** Mining Consortium
- **Common Names/Nicknames:** The Guild, The Pits, The Diggers
- **Faction Type:** Labor union federation / Worker-controlled territory
- **Founded:** ~2092 (guild consolidation post-Renewal)
- **Status:** Active, vulnerable to economic pressure
- **Estimated Population:** ~170,000

### Territory & Influence
- **Primary Territory:** Southern territories (Saint-Constant, Delson, La Prairie areas)
- **Secondary Areas:** Strip mines, ore fields, processing facilities
- **Territorial Borders:** 
  - East: Greenfield Dominion
  - North: Stacks Federation (scattered enclaves)
  - South/West: Border March
- **Key Locations:** 
  - Saint-Constant Mining Stack (17 units high, defensive, ~100,000 population)
  - The Pits (general mining area, multiple sites)
  - Ore Fields (rare earth extraction)
  - The Foundries (mineral refinement centers)
  - Copper Banks (metal extraction)
  - Del's Son/Delson (mining equipment depot)
  - The Prairie/La Prairie (atmospheric processor farms)
  - Carig Station/Carignan (rail freight terminus)
  - Chamber City/Chambly (underground bunker complex)
  - Kyr4n's Research Lab (secret, in abandoned mine—not Consortium controlled)
- **Sphere of Influence:** Resource extraction, rare earth supply, heavy equipment operations

## Leadership & Organization

- **Type:** Guild Council (union leaders and mine bosses)

- **Structure:** Democratic in theory, oligarchic in practice

## Values, Culture & Goals

### Core Values
- **Worker Protection:** Safety and fair treatment (relative to alternatives)
- **Pragmatic Cooperation:** Union-management balance
- **Resource Control:** Monopoly on rare earth extraction
- **Collective Strength:** Unity through necessity
- **Fair Day's Work:** Honest labor deserves honest pay

### Cultural Identity
- **Language/Dialect:** Franglais Patois with mining terminology
- **Customs & Traditions:** 
  - Guild meetings and voting (somewhat democratic)
  - Worker safety rituals (learned through tragedy)
  - Solidarity among miners
  - Respect for experience and skill
- **Internal Culture:** 
  - Pragmatic worker ethos
  - Tired of exploitation
  - Strong union traditions
  - Mine boss pragmatism
  - Chemical refining expertise
  - Pollution normalization
- **Public Perception:** 
  - Internally: Our work keeps the Shore alive
  - Externally: Essential but exploited
  - Reputation: Tough workers, valuable resources, vulnerable to pressure

### Primary Goals
1. **Short-term:** Maintain mining operations, protect worker conditions
2. **Medium-term:** Improve compensation, reduce exploitation
3. **Long-term:** Find leadership that values workers
4. **Ultimate Vision:** Fair treatment for essential labor

## Assets & Capabilities

### Economic Resources
- **Revenue Sources:** 
  - Rare earth extraction (primary income)
  - Metal refining and processing
  - Heavy equipment operations
  - Chemical refinement (dependencies on Greenfield)
  - Raw material sales to all territories
- **Annual Income:** Substantial gross, but heavily extracted by others
- **Economic Model:** Labor-based production, vulnerable to buyer power
- **Trade Relationships:** 
  - Sell to all territories (essential supplier)
  - Dependent on Greenfield for chemical inputs
  - Guild merchant relationships
  - Equipment purchases from various sources

### Military/Security Capability
- **Fighting Force Size:** ~200-300 defenders (workers who can fight)
- **Equipment Level:** Industrial tools as weapons, limited military gear
- **Training Quality:** Workers, not soldiers; some combat experience
- **Defensive Assets:** 
  - Mining equipment as weapons
  - Explosives expertise
  - Territory knowledge (mines, tunnels)
  - Fortified stack settlements
- **Offensive Capability:** Limited; focused on defense

### Technological Resources
- **Tech Level:** Industrial specialization
- **Specialized Technology:** 
  - Mining equipment (drills, explosives, heavy machinery)
  - Ore processing and refinement
  - Geothermal energy capture (vents provide warmth)
  - Underground engineering
- **Research & Development:** Practical improvements only
- **Tech Advantages:** Resource extraction expertise, explosives knowledge

### Infrastructure
- **Facilities:** 
  - Multiple mining sites (The Pits)
  - Processing and refinement facilities
  - Equipment depots
  - Rail freight terminal
  - Underground bunker complexes
  - Worker housing (defensive stacks)
- **Communication Systems:** Guild coordination networks
- **Transportation:** 
  - Rail systems (Carig Station)
  - Heavy equipment vehicles
  - Tunnel networks
- **Supply Lines:** Dependent on external food/goods, self-sufficient in minerals
