# Northside Collective

> *"Five tyrants in constant conflict—brutal, practical, and always teetering on the edge of civil war."*

---

## Basic Information

### Identity
- **Official Name:** Northside Collective
- **Common Names/Nicknames:** The Council, Northside, The Five, The Industrial Barons
- **Faction Type:** Gang confederacy / Territorial coalition
- **Founded:** ~2090s (gradual formation from post-Renewal gang wars)
- **Status:** Conquered by Phoenix Rising (~2132, Era 5)
- **Estimated Population:** ~200,000 (largest single South Shore territory before Phoenix Rising expansion)

### Territory & Influence
- **Primary Territory:** Northern industrial zones (Longueuil-Nord and Brossard areas)
- **Secondary Areas:** Heavy manufacturing districts, logistics corridors
- **Territorial Borders:** 
  - South: Phoenix Rising
  - East: Riverside Sovereignty
  - Outer edges: Border March
  - Internal: Five semi-independent gang territories
- **Key Locations:** 
  - Brossard Industrial Stack (18 units high, distributed clusters, ~120,000 population)
  - New Brossard (middle management housing with sewage)
  - The Yards (factories and shipping facilities)
  - Industrial Scrapyards (metal and electronic waste mountains)
  - Power Line Networks (electrical tangles for jury-rigged energy)
  - Airstrip Market (old runway trading hub near transport)
- **Sphere of Influence:** Northern industrial production, manufacturing output for South Shore

## Leadership & Organization

### Leadership Structure
- **Type:** Unstable coalition / Council of equals (in theory)
- **Current Leader(s):** Council of Five gang leaders (names TBD in canon)
- **Leadership History:** 
  - Post-Renewal (~2090s): Five gangs emerge from territorial wars
  - Uneasy coalition forms to prevent mutual destruction
  - Constant power struggles and shifting alliances
  - ~2132: Aggression against Greenfield triggers Phoenix Rising intervention
  - ~2132: Conquered and absorbed by Phoenix Rising
- **Succession Method:** Violence; each gang leader maintains power through force, council membership through controlling a territory

### Organizational Hierarchy
- **Council of Five:** Five equal (theoretically) gang leaders
- **Gang Lieutenants:** Second-tier enforcers within each gang
- **Enforcers:** Muscle and street-level controllers
- **Workers:** Industrial laborers, pressured for tribute
- **Internal Structure:** Five parallel hierarchies in constant tension

### Key Figures
- **Council of Five** - Gang Leaders: Five brutal bosses, names TBD, constantly jockeying for dominance
- **Cutter** - Former Northside Enforcer (defected): Escaped 5 years before Era 5, joined Phoenix Rising, rose to squad leader

## Values, Culture & Goals

### Core Values
- **Strength through intimidation:** Power comes from fear
- **Practical brutality:** Violence is a tool, not personal
- **Territorial control:** Each gang's domain is sacred
- **Industrial output:** Production capacity equals power
- **No weakness:** Any sign of softness is exploited

**Internal Contradictions:**
- Five equal leaders inherently unstable
- Brutality alienates workforce needed for production
- Constant internal conflict weakens external position
- Short-term extraction undermines long-term sustainability

### Cultural Identity
- **Language/Dialect:** Franglais Patois with industrial slang
- **Customs & Traditions:** 
  - Council meetings (rare, tense, often violent)
  - Territory boundaries rigidly enforced internally
  - Worker exploitation normalized
  - Might makes right
- **Internal Culture:** 
  - Brutal but practical
  - Constant power struggles
  - Labor vs. management tension
  - Union-fractured (labor power exists but fragmented)
  - Gritty industrial aesthetic
- **Public Perception:** 
  - Internally: Feared oppressors
  - Externally: Dangerous but predictable
  - Reputation: Largest territory but weakest unity

### Primary Goals
1. **Short-term:** Maintain territorial control, extract maximum production
2. **Medium-term:** Expand territory, especially into Greenfield
3. **Long-term:** Unclear—no unified vision beyond survival
4. **Ultimate Vision:** Each Council member wants sole dominance; collective has no coherent goal

## Assets & Capabilities

### Economic Resources
- **Revenue Sources:** 
  - Industrial production (heavy manufacturing)
  - Logistics and shipping
  - Worker exploitation/tribute
  - Scrapyard salvage operations
  - Black market metal and component sales
- **Annual Income:** High gross revenue, but divided five ways and inefficiently managed
- **Economic Model:** Extraction-based; short-term profits over sustainability
- **Trade Relationships:** Sell industrial output to other territories, Guild merchants (reluctantly)

### Military/Security Capability
- **Fighting Force Size:** Largest territory = largest gang force (collective ~500-800 fighters)
- **Equipment Level:** Gang-level (salvaged and improvised)
  - Conventional firearms
  - Industrial tools as weapons
  - Limited body armor
  - Crude but effective
- **Training Quality:** Variable; experienced street fighters but no military discipline
- **Defensive Assets:** 
  - Industrial fortifications (factories as strongholds)
  - Numbers advantage
  - Territory knowledge
  - Power line networks complicate intrusion
- **Offensive Capability:** 
  - Can muster large raiding parties
  - Industrial equipment as weapons
  - Numerical superiority
  - But: poor coordination between gangs

### Technological Resources
- **Tech Level:** Industrial-age; heavy on mechanical, light on digital
- **Specialized Technology:** 
  - Heavy manufacturing equipment
  - Industrial lifters and vehicles
  - Power generation and distribution (jury-rigged)
  - Scrapyard salvage operations
- **Research & Development:** None; purely pragmatic adaptation
- **Tech Advantages:** Industrial capacity exceeds most territories

### Infrastructure
- **Facilities:** 
  - Multiple factory complexes
  - Shipping and logistics hubs
  - Scrapyard processing facilities
  - Worker housing (poor condition)
  - Council meeting neutral zones
- **Communication Systems:** Basic; each gang has own network, minimal coordination
- **Transportation:** 
  - Airstrip for cargo
  - Road networks through industrial zones
  - Internal transportation fragmented by gang territories
- **Supply Lines:** Self-sufficient in industrial goods, import food and other necessities

## Methods & Operations

### Standard Operating Procedures
- Council meetings only when absolutely necessary
- Each gang operates independently in their territory
- Disputes settled through shows of force
- Workers kept in line through intimidation
- External threats sometimes unite the Five (temporarily)

### Tactics & Strategy
- **Military Tactics:** 
  - Overwhelming numbers
  - Brutal intimidation
  - Industrial equipment as weapons
  - Territory defense in depth (fortified factories)
  - Poor inter-gang coordination
  
- **Political Strategy:** 
  - Minimal; primarily focused on maintaining internal balance
  - External aggression (Greenfield) attempted to unite gangs
  - Failed to anticipate Phoenix Rising response
  
- **Economic Methods:** 
  - Maximum extraction from workers
  - Selling industrial output
  - Scrapyard salvage and resale
  - No investment in sustainability

### Reputation & Approach
- **Public Image:** Brutal oppressors, dangerous neighbors
- **Self-Presentation:** Strong, unified (contradicted by reality)
- **Reality:** Five gangs in unstable coalition, constant internal tension
- **Gap:** Claim of unity vs. reality of fragmentation; size implies strength but structure implies weakness

## Historical Timeline

### Formation Era (~2090s)
- Post-Renewal gang wars create territorial chaos
- Five dominant gangs emerge from violence
- Mutual destruction threat forces coalition
- Council of Five establishes uneasy power-sharing

### Consolidation Era (~2090s-2120s)
- Industrial focus develops (factories, shipping, scrapyards)
- Worker exploitation becomes systematic
- Territory grows to 200,000 population (largest pre-Phoenix)
- Internal power struggles constant but contained
- External borders stabilize through fear

### Decline Era (~2125-2132)
- Phoenix Rising's success threatens exploitation model
- Internal tensions increase as example of better governance spreads
- Attempted expansion into Greenfield to unite gangs
- Aggression triggers Phoenix Rising intervention (defending Greenfield)
- Council fails to coordinate effective defense

### Conquest (~2132, Era 5)
- Phoenix Rising intervention in Greenfield conflict
- Systematic conquest of Northside territories
- Kyr4n's intelligence advantage: hacked Northside communications completely
- Dual-objective operation: defend Greenfield + seize Northside sectors
- Council fragmented under pressure
- Workers welcome Phoenix Rising as liberators
- Territory absorbed into Phoenix Rising

### Integration (Post-2132)
- Former Northside territory becomes Phoenix Rising's largest expansion
- Workers receive better conditions, many integrate willingly
- Some enforcers flee, others integrate (Cutter's backstory as model)
- Industrial capacity adds to Phoenix Rising's economic base
- Strategic consolidation continues under new management

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Advantages
1. **Size:** Largest territory population (200,000)
2. **Industrial Capacity:** Heavy manufacturing and production capability
3. **Numbers:** Can field large fighting forces
4. **Territory Knowledge:** Defensive advantage in industrial complexes
5. **Fear Factor:** Reputation for brutality deters some aggression

### Vulnerabilities
1. **Leadership Instability:** Five leaders in constant tension
2. **Poor Coordination:** Gang structure prevents unified action
3. **Worker Resentment:** Oppressed population won't defend oppressors
4. **No Innovation:** Purely extraction, no development or improvement
5. **External Isolation:** No allies, only trading partners
6. **Predictable:** Brutal but unimaginative tactics
7. **Technology Gap:** Gang-level equipment vs. Phoenix Rising's near-military grade

### Internal Conflicts
- Constant power struggles between Council members
- Worker rebellions suppressed but simmering
- Territory boundaries within Collective rigidly enforced
- No unified strategy or vision
- Each gang prioritizes self-interest over collective good
- Leadership turnover through violence creates instability
