## Basic Information

### Identity
- **Official Name:** Greenfield Dominion
- **Common Names/Nicknames:** The Chemists, Greenfield, The Families
- **Faction Type:** Chemical cartel / Family-controlled territory
- **Founded:** ~2093 (three families consolidate control)
- **Status:** Active, entered defensive alliance with Phoenix Rising after Northside aggression
- **Estimated Population:** ~150,000

### Territory & Influence
- **Primary Territory:** Southeast agro-industrial zones (Sainte-Julie/Saint-Amable areas)
- **Secondary Areas:** Toxic waste sites, pharmaceutical plants, processing facilities
- **Territorial Borders:** 
  - North: Riverside Sovereignty
  - West: Mining Consortium
  - South/East: Border March
  - Under threat: Northside Collective aggression (Era 5)
- **Key Locations:** 
  - Greenfield Waste Stack (16 units high, defensive, ~80,000 population)
  - Greenfield Wastes (toxic dumping grounds, green glow at night)
  - Candy-ac/Candiac (pharmaceutical manufacturing)
  - Otter Burn/Otterburn Park (waste incineration)
  - Mac's Town/McMasterville (corporate middle management housing)
  - Mary's Ville/Marieville (agricultural processing)
  - Catherine's Ward/Sainte-Catherine (medical district)
  - Constant City/Saint-Constant (housing blocks)
  - Izzy's Point/Saint-Isidore (information broker territory)
- **Sphere of Influence:** Drug manufacturing, chemical processing, hazmat operations, pharmaceutical production

## Leadership & Organization

### Leadership Structure
- **Type:** Family cartel (three dominant families)

  - Post-Renewal: Chemical industry collapse leaves equipment

  - ~2132 (Era 5): Northside aggression forces Phoenix Rising alliance

### Organizational Hierarchy
- **Three Family Heads:** Co-leaders of Chemist Collective

- **Chemists/Scientists:** Technical experts and production managers
- **Enforcers/Guards:** Security with chemical weapons training
- **Workers:** Production staff, often with hazmat exposure
- **Structure:** Three parallel family hierarchies requiring coordination

## Values, Culture & Goals

### Core Values
- **Scientific Excellence:** Chemical mastery is power
- **Family Loyalty:** Blood ties above all
- **Paranoid Security:** Trust no outsiders
- **Environmental Control:** Weaponize the environment itself
- **Monopoly Protection:** Chemical production monopoly must be defended

### Cultural Identity
- **Language/Dialect:** Franglais Patois with technical chemical terminology
- **Customs & Traditions:** 
  - Family secrets closely guarded
  - Scientific achievement celebrated
  - Hazmat operations normalized
  - Green glow of wastes as territorial marker
  - Chemical expertise as status
- **Internal Culture:** 
  - Ruthless but scientifically minded
  - Paranoid about external threats
  - Three families cooperate uneasily
  - Pride in chemical sophistication
  - Hazmat operations routine
  - Environmental destruction accepted as business cost
- **Public Perception:** 
  - Internally: Brilliant survivors in hostile environment
  - Externally: Dangerous, unpredictable, toxic
  - Reputation: Most dangerous in direct conflict due to chemical weapons

### Primary Goals
1. **Short-term:** Maintain chemical production monopoly, defend territory
2. **Medium-term:** Expand pharmaceutical markets, improve defenses
3. **Long-term:** Remain independent through chemical weapons capability
4. **Ultimate Vision:** Chemical mastery guarantees survival and power

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Advantages
1. **Chemical Weapons:** Most dangerous military capability on Shore
2. **Scientific Expertise:** Pharmaceutical and chemical mastery
3. **Defensive Terrain:** Toxic environment protects territory
4. **Monopoly Position:** Essential chemical/pharmaceutical supplier
5. **Three-Family Alliance:** Multiple leadership prevents single-point failure
6. **Environmental Weaponization:** Can deny entire areas

### Vulnerabilities
1. **Paranoid Isolation:** Few allies until forced by circumstance
2. **Environmental Destruction:** Poisoning own territory
3. **Three-Way Tension:** Family cooperation fragile
4. **Hazmat Dependency:** Population requires protective equipment
5. **Mutual Destruction Risk:** Chemical weapons can be turned against them
6. **Smaller Population:** 150,000 vs. larger territories

### Internal Conflicts
- Three families compete while cooperating
- Scientific excellence vs. environmental destruction
- Paranoid isolation vs. need for allies
- Chemical weapon capability vs. mutual destruction risk
- Family loyalty vs. collective survival
