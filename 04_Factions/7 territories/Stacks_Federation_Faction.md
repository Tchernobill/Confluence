## Basic Information

### Identity
- **Official Name:** Stacks Federation
- **Common Names/Nicknames:** The Federation, The Independents, Scattered Stacks
- **Faction Type:** Loose federation / Decentralized settlements
- **Founded:** ~2088 (post-Renewal autonomous organization)
- **Status:** Active but fragmented, gradually being absorbed
- **Estimated Population:** ~450,000 (largest total but scattered across 15+ settlements)

### Territory & Influence
- **Primary Territory:** Scattered suburbs (Boucherville, Chambly, Carignan, Saint-Basile areas)
- **Secondary Areas:** Multiple independent enclaves throughout Shore
- **Territorial Borders:** 
  - Enclaves touch all other territories
  - Especially Phoenix Rising (central) and Mining Consortium (south)
  - Distributed rather than contiguous territory
- **Key Locations:** 
  - Chambly Federation Stack (20 units high, scattered clusters, ~250,000 total across enclaves)
  - Neighborhood Stacks (multiple 5-10 block clusters)
  - Bruno Heights/Saint-Bruno-de-Montarville (elevated shantytown)
  - Hilary's Mount/Mont-Saint-Hilaire (observation post/radio towers)
  - The Monk/LeMoyne (religious resistance hideout)
  - Lambert's Gate/Saint-Lambert (border checkpoint)
  - Rural Outposts (mining/agricultural support bases)
  - Tunnel Town (metro extensions, black market)
  - The Deep (lower levels, hidden communities)
  - Pipe City (service tunnels, worker settlements)
  - The Maze (passages, smuggling network)
- **Sphere of Influence:** Decentralized resistance to unification, autonomous self-sufficient communities

## Values, Culture & Goals

### Core Values
- **Independence:** Autonomy above all, resist external control
- **Self-Sufficiency:** Each neighborhood provides for itself
- **Local Identity:** Neighborhood loyalty stronger than federation
- **Survival-Focused:** Practical concerns over ideology
- **Anti-Unification:** Suspicious of centralized power

### Cultural Identity
- **Language/Dialect:** Franglais Patois varying by settlement
- **Customs & Traditions:** 
  - Each neighborhood has distinct culture
  - Local decision-making paramount
  - Federation meetings rare and contentious
  - Self-reliance celebrated
  - Diversity of approaches (religious, secular, militant, peaceful)
- **Internal Culture:** 
  - Independent, survival-focused
  - Resistant to outside control
  - Semi-autonomous enclaves
  - No unified military or communication
  - Local solutions to local problems
- **Public Perception:** 
  - Internally: Proud independence
  - Externally: Fragmented and weak
  - Reputation: Strong locally, weak collectively

### Primary Goals
1. **Short-term:** Maintain neighborhood independence
2. **Medium-term:** Survival through self-sufficiency
3. **Long-term:** Preserve autonomy despite consolidation pressures
4. **Ultimate Vision:** Federation of truly independent communities (increasingly unrealistic)

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Advantages
1. **Large Population:** 450,000 total (largest in aggregate)
2. **Distributed:** Hard to destroy all at once
3. **Self-Sufficient:** Each neighborhood survives independently
4. **Diverse:** Multiple approaches and solutions
5. **Local Knowledge:** Each area understood by residents

### Vulnerabilities
1. **No Unity:** Can't coordinate effective resistance
2. **No Unified Military:** Each neighborhood defends alone
3. **Poor Communication:** Fragmented information sharing
4. **Easily Isolated:** Picked off one at a time
5. **Ideological Weakness:** Independence insufficient against better governance
6. **No Central Resources:** Can't pool strength

### Internal Conflicts
- Neighborhood competition for resources
- Federation vs. local authority
- Unity vs. independence debates
- Practical needs vs. ideological purity
- Coordination attempts fail repeatedly
